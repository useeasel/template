Lina Marsh is a painter based in the Pacific Northwest. Her work moves between
oil on canvas and gouache on paper, building compositions from simple shapes —
the circle, the square, the triangle — and a deliberately limited palette.

Her practice is rooted in observation: light across a wall, the edge where two
colors meet, the weight of a single form on a field of paper. Recent series have
turned toward larger formats and bolder color contrasts.

She studied painting at the regional art college and has exhibited in group and
solo shows across the region since 2016.
